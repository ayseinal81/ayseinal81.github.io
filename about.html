I'm an instructional designer and e-learning developer with 20+ years in digital learning and a background spanning law, compliance, tech, and operational training. That range is my advantage: I've sat close enough to the business to know what "good" actually looks like on the job.

I specialise in building programmes that are creative, evidence-based, and measurable from day one. I lean on gamification, scenario-based design, and blended approaches — then I use the Kirkpatrick model to prove they work. Reaction and knowledge are the starting line, not the finish.

I'm a TechWomen100 finalist, hold an MA in Education & Technology from UCL, and I'm a lifelong advocate for learning that changes behaviour rather than simply getting completed.

How I approach it

1

Start with the behaviour. Before a single screen, we agree what should change on the job — and how we'll know it did.

2

Design for decisions, not clicks. Scenarios and games put learners in realistic pressure so practice happens before it matters.

3

Prove it with evidence. Kirkpatrick from day one, so the programme earns its place instead of just filling a slot.
